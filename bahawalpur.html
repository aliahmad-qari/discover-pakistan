Bahawalpur: The Majestic City of Nawabs

Updated December 2024 Punjab Province, Pakistan

Historical Background

Bahawalpur, founded in 1748 by Nawab Bahawal Khan Abbasi, was once the capital of the princely state of Bahawalpur. The city is renowned for its royal heritage, magnificent palaces, and rich Siraiki culture. It remained an independent state until 1955 when it was merged with Pakistan.

The Abbasi dynasty ruled Bahawalpur for over 200 years, leaving behind a legacy of architectural marvels and cultural traditions that continue to define the city's identity today.

Famous Places & Landmarks

  • Noor Mahal: Magnificent palace built in Italian style
  • Derawar Fort: 40-tower fortress in Cholistan Desert
  • Darbar Mahal: Former royal residence and museum
  • Gulzar Mahal: Beautiful palace with intricate architecture
  • Tomb of Bibi Jawindi: 15th-century mausoleum
  • Cholistan Desert: Vast desert with ancient forts
  • Lal Suhanra National Park: Wildlife sanctuary and biosphere reserve

Culture & Traditions

Bahawalpur is the heart of Siraiki culture, where the melodious Siraiki language and traditions flourish. The city is famous for its Siraiki poetry, folk music, and traditional crafts including pottery, embroidery, and camel skin products.

The annual Shalimar Festival celebrates the region's cultural heritage with traditional music, dance, and handicrafts. The city's royal past is reflected in its refined cultural practices and hospitality.

Food & Cuisine

Bahawalpur offers a unique blend of Siraiki and royal cuisine:

  • Siraiki Karahi: Traditional meat curry with local spices
  • Sohan Halwa: Famous sweet delicacy of Bahawalpur
  • Kheer Puri: Traditional breakfast combination
  • Roasted Lamb: Royal-style meat preparation
  • Multani Mitti Sweets: Local confectionery specialties

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Bahawalpur is from October to March when the weather is pleasant for exploring palaces and desert areas. Winter months offer comfortable temperatures for visiting Cholistan Desert and outdoor historical sites. The Shalimar Festival in February is particularly recommended for cultural enthusiasts.

Quick Facts

  • Population: ~800,000
  • Founded: 1748
  • Language: Siraiki, Urdu
