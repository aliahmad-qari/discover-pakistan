Islamabad - Capital City

Pakistan's modern planned capital nestled in the Margalla Hills

Travel Capital Modern City Government

by Real Pakistan ‚Ä¢ January 15, 2026

Islamabad, Pakistan's purpose-built capital city, represents the country's modern aspirations. Planned in the 1960s and nestled against the scenic Margalla Hills, it combines contemporary urban planning with natural beauty, serving as the seat of government and diplomacy.

Historical Background

Islamabad was conceived in the 1960s to replace Karachi as Pakistan's capital. The city was designed by Greek architect Constantinos Apostolou Doxiadis, following modern urban planning principles.

Construction began in 1961, and the city officially became the capital in 1967. The name "Islamabad" means "City of Islam," reflecting the country's Islamic identity and values.
