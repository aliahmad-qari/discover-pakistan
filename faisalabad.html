Faisalabad - Manchester of Pakistan

Industrial powerhouse and textile capital of Pakistan

Travel Punjab Industrial City Textile Hub

by Real Pakistan ‚Ä¢ January 15, 2026

Faisalabad, known as the "Manchester of Pakistan," is the country's third-largest city and industrial capital. Famous for its textile industry and agricultural production, it represents Pakistan's economic strength and entrepreneurial spirit.

Historical Background

Originally founded as Lyallpur in 1890 by the British, the city was planned as an agricultural center. It was renamed Faisalabad in 1977 after King Faisal of Saudi Arabia.

The city developed rapidly due to its strategic location in the fertile plains of Punjab and became Pakistan's major textile and industrial hub, earning the nickname "Manchester of Pakistan."
