Peshawar - Gateway to Khyber

Ancient city with rich Gandhara heritage and vibrant Pashtun culture

Travel KPK Ancient City Pashtun Culture

by Real Pakistan ‚Ä¢ January 15, 2026

Peshawar, one of the oldest cities in South Asia, serves as the gateway to the historic Khyber Pass. With over 2,000 years of history, this ancient city has been a crossroads of civilizations, showcasing rich Gandhara heritage and vibrant Pashtun culture.

Historical Background

Peshawar's history dates back to ancient times when it was known as Purushapura during the Gandhara civilization. The city has been ruled by various empires including the Kushans, Mughals, Sikhs, and British.

As the capital of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Peshawar has always been strategically important due to its location near the Khyber Pass, connecting South Asia with Central Asia and Afghanistan.

Culture & Traditions

Peshawar's rich Pashtun culture includes:

  • Language: Pashto, Urdu
  • Traditions: Pashtunwali code of conduct
  • Arts: Traditional music and dance
  • Crafts: Carpet weaving, metalwork
  • Heritage: Gandhara Buddhist civilization
