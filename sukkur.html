Sukkur: The Historic City on River Indus

Updated December 2024 Sindh Province, Pakistan

Historical Background

Sukkur is one of the oldest cities in Sindh, strategically located on the west bank of the River Indus. The city has been an important trading center for centuries, serving as a gateway between upper and lower Sindh. Its history dates back to ancient times when it was known as Aror, the capital of Sindh.

The famous Lansdowne Bridge, built in 1889, connects Sukkur with Rohri and stands as a testament to British engineering. The city played a crucial role during the British colonial period as a major railway junction and river port.

Famous Places & Landmarks

  • Lansdowne Bridge: Historic cantilever bridge over River Indus
  • Sukkur Barrage: Major irrigation project completed in 1932
  • Sadh Belo Temple: Hindu temple on an island in River Indus
  • Masum Shah Minaret: 14th-century historical monument
  • Sukkur Museum: Showcases local history and artifacts
  • Clock Tower: British-era landmark in the city center

Culture & Traditions

Sukkur represents the rich Sindhi culture with its traditional architecture, handicrafts, and customs. The city is known for its Sindhi Ajrak and Topi, traditional clothing that reflects the cultural identity of the region.

The people of Sukkur are known for their hospitality and maintain strong connections to their Sindhi heritage. Traditional festivals like Sawan and cultural celebrations are observed with great enthusiasm.

Food & Cuisine

Sukkur offers authentic Sindhi cuisine with specialties including:

  • Sindhi Biryani: Aromatic rice dish with unique Sindhi spices
  • Sai Bhaji: Traditional spinach and lentil curry
  • Koki: Sindhi flatbread with onions and spices
  • Sindhi Curry: Tangy vegetable curry
  • Seyal Mani: Leftover bread cooked with tomatoes

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Sukkur is during the winter months from November to March when the weather is pleasant and suitable for sightseeing. The temperature during this period ranges from 15°C to 25°C, making it ideal for exploring the city's historical sites and enjoying boat rides on the River Indus.

Quick Facts
  • Province: Sindh
  • Population: ~500,000
  • Founded: Ancient times
  • Language: Sindhi, Urdu
  • Famous For: Lansdowne Bridge
  • River: Indus
