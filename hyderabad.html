Hyderabad - Paris of Sindh

Historic city with rich Sindhi culture and traditional crafts

Travel Sindh Historic City Sindhi Culture

by Real Pakistan ‚Ä¢ January 15, 2026

Hyderabad, known as the "Paris of Sindh," is a historic city founded by the Talpur rulers. Famous for its rich Sindhi culture, traditional crafts, and beautiful architecture, it serves as an important cultural center of Sindh province.

Historical Background

Founded in 1768 by Mian Ghulam Shah Kalhoro, Hyderabad became the capital of Sindh under the Talpur dynasty. The city was strategically built on the east bank of the River Indus.

Hyderabad flourished as a center of trade, culture, and learning. The city is known for its beautiful palaces, gardens, and traditional Sindhi architecture that reflects its glorious past.

Culture & Traditions

Hyderabad's rich Sindhi culture includes:

  • Language: Sindhi, Urdu
  • Crafts: Ajrak, Sindhi caps, embroidery
  • Music: Traditional Sindhi folk songs
  • Literature: Sindhi poetry and literature
  • Festivals: Cheti Chand, Sawan
