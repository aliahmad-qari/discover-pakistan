Hyderabad

Paris of Sindh

City Introduction

Hyderabad is a historical Sindhi city known for its rich culture, traditional crafts, and beautiful architecture. It is the second-largest city of Sindh province.

Historical Background

Founded by the Talpur rulers in 1768. The city served as the capital of Sindh before British rule and has a rich history of Sindhi culture and traditions.

Culture & Society

Strong Sindhi culture with emphasis on arts, crafts, and literature. People are known for their peaceful nature, love for poetry, and traditional handicrafts like ajrak and sindhi topi.

Lifestyle & Traditions

Traditional Sindhi living with focus on family values and cultural preservation. People celebrate traditional festivals and maintain their ancestral customs and language.

Famous Places

  • Pacco Qillo (Hyderabad Fort) - Historic fort
  • Sindh Museum - Cultural heritage museum
  • Tombs of Talpur Mirs - Historic royal tombs

Language & Local Food

Sindhi is the main language. Famous for Sindhi biryani, sai bhaji, and traditional sweets. The city offers authentic Sindhi cuisine with unique flavors and cooking styles.

Special Cultural Highlight

Traditional ajrak (block-printed shawl) and Sindhi cap (topi) are symbols of Sindhi culture. The city is famous for its handicrafts, mirror work, and traditional Sindhi embroidery.
