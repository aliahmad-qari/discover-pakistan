Dera Ghazi Khan

Heart of Siraiki Culture

City Introduction

Dera Ghazi Khan is a pure Siraiki city that represents the authentic Siraiki culture, language, and traditions. It is the cultural heart of the Siraiki-speaking region.

Historical Background

Founded by Ghazi Khan Mirrani in the 15th century. The city has been the center of Siraiki culture for centuries and has maintained its unique identity and traditions throughout history.

Culture & Society

Strong Siraiki culture with emphasis on poetry, music, and hospitality. People are known for their warmth, simplicity, and deep connection to their Siraiki heritage and language.

Lifestyle & Traditions

Simple and tribal lifestyle with strong community bonds. People follow traditional Siraiki customs, enjoy folk music and poetry, and maintain their ancestral way of life.

Traditional Villages

Language & Local Food

Pure Siraiki language is spoken, preserving the authentic regional dialect. Traditional foods include roti, saag (leafy vegetables), and simple but nutritious rural cuisine that reflects the agricultural lifestyle.

Special Cultural Highlight

Pure Siraiki language and customs make this city unique. It is the center of Siraiki literature, folk music, and traditional crafts. The city preserves the most authentic form of Siraiki culture in Pakistan.
